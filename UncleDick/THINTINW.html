The Hut in the Tree in the Woods

What it is about...

From New York Times best-sellig author Richard M. Siddoway comes The Hut in the Tree in the Woods, a hilarious tale of two young boys and their escapades in the woods behind their house. Bill and Dick are cousins, and, according to Bill, partners in crime. But mostly, Dick just goes along with whatever Bill says as the two of them and their gang of friends -- all eight-ten-year-old-boys -- plan great adventures from their hut in the woods.

Delightfully told from a grown-up Dick's point of view, this short novel tells a clever tale of the lessons learned from thsoe soemtimes ridiculous caper of youth

From staging an Indian attack during a Pioneer Days parade through 1950s Salt Lake City to tunneling through mounds of snow on Roosevelt Avenue, the boys get into a load of trouble. The also have a lot of fun. Many of their escapades will leave readers rolling with laughter and recalling the days of their youth, days when the most fun was usually had with only a friend and a wild imgination in tow. The Hut in the Tree in the Woods will delight readers of all ages and will leave everyone rolling with laughter as they get to know Dick and his wild cousin Bill.
